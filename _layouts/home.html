Greetings, and welcome to Collin O'Connor's professional and personal website!

Professionally,

      Collin O'Connor is a spatial epidemiologist, ecologist, and biogeographer, primarly focused on researching the dynamic mechanisms of tick-borne disease eco-epidemiology. A recent Ph.D. graduate from the University at Buffalo's Department of Geography, Collin spent five years taking courses in spatial statistical and econometric analysis, spatial data science, biostatistics, and geostatistics, all under the tutelage of his academic advisor, Jared Aldstadt. He also created and taught two spatial statistics courses for undergraduates: Univariate Statistics in Geography and Multivariate Statistics in Geography, each of which included a laboratory section teaching basic R programming and some spatial features.
      Prior to his Ph.D. journey, Collin earned a Master of Science in epidemiology and a Bachelor of Science in biology from the University at Albany. Go Danes! And he even spent a semester abroad taking courses towards his biology degree at the University of Galway, in Galway City, Ireland!
      Outside of academia, Collin works for the New York State Department of Health (NYSDOH) in the Bureau of Communicable Disease Control. Specifically, Collin is a member of the NYSDOH Vector Ecology Laboratory, headed by Melissa Prusinski. The research conducted in the Vector Ecology Laboratory involves traveling to public parks throughout New York State to collect live tick specimens and test them for potential pathogens. This research has guided Collin's professional and academic journey, culminating in several publications and a dissertation: Deer as Dilution Hosts: Testing Density Dependent Mechanisms in the Ixodes scapularis and Anaplasma phagocytophilum Genetic Variant System.
